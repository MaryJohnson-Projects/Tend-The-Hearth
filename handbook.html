Character Stats

Attributes

Characters within Tend the Hearth have 3 attributes that they use to contend with the various trials they face. When creating a character, you must assign the numbers 1, 2, and 3 each to a single attribute. This represents a character's aptitude with an attribute, the higher the better. When you take an action that requires an attribute roll, you roll a number of d10 equal to your attribute value.

Power

Power represents a character's active physical ability. It is strength, finesse, or even just endurance while performing arduous tasks. This attribute is rolled during the Gather Materials action.

  • A character with a 3 in Power might be physically strong. They might be slow but steady. They might have the dexterity to recover from issues that crop up.
  • A character with a 1 in Power might be physically weak. They might not have the stamina to perform taxing tasks for long stretches of time. They might be clumsy.
Resilience

Resilience represents a character's passive physical ability, think of it as your health, body, or even luck resisting ailments. This attribute is rolled during the Endure Conditions action.

  • A character with a 3 in Resilience might have a natural affinity for the environment that they find themselves in, they might be used to difficult conditions, they might just be lucky.
  • A character with a 1 in Resilience might have a poor immune system. They might be ill prepared for the environment their group is within. They might not have the best constitution.
Judgement

Judgement represents a character's mental acuity and ability to make good decisions. It is the ability to plan, see opportunities, and innovate in the field. This attribute is rolled curing the Reconstruction action.

  • A character with a 3 in Judgement might be a great problem solver. They might have the appropriate level of caution for the situation. They might have familiarity with survival.
  • A character with a 1 in Judgement might be reckless. They might be filled with bad ideas. They might panic under pressure.

Counters

Characters within Tend the Hearth have 3 individual counters and 1 global counter that tracks the state of your character and the world around you.

Fatigue

Fatigue is a measure of how the elements wear you down. All character's fatigue counters start at 0. Characters tend to gain fatigue whenever they suffer the effects of the harsh environment around them. Fatigue is subtracted from all of the rolls you are involved with.

  • Your Fatigue cannot be negative.
  • When a character is at 3 Fatigue or higher, they can only use the Rest action.
  • When a character's Fatigue counter reaches 6 or higher, they may elect to retreat from the expedition permanently. They will survive, but they cannot return to the expedition.
  • If a character's Fatigue counter rises to 7 or more, they die.
Rested

Your character is either Rested or not rested. You may Rest to fulfill the Rested condition. All characters start the game with their Rested condition unfulfilled. If a character is not Rested at Dawn, they take the Endure Conditions reaction. All characters will have their Rested status set to unfulfilled after the Dawn step.

Days

The GM will keep track of how many days have passed. This counter causes GM actions to become more severe. It is important to be quick in your efforts, however it is more important to stay safe.

Resources

Resources are needed to reconstruct, but they are also needed to survive. A group of characters start with 1 Food per character and 1 wood as a group. This ensures that the group can spend the first day preparing for the wilderness.

Food

The food resource represents rations and hunted game to keep the party sustained. One ration is consumed per character per day at Dawn. Food is a volatile resource, it is subject to being destroyed through failure or bad luck.

Wood

The wood resource represents logs and sticks gathered from the forest nearby. One wood is consumed at every Dawn to stoke the fire. Wood is a volatile resource, it is subject to being destroyed through failure or bad luck.

Stone

The stone resource represents large stones and small rocks gathered from the wilderness. Stone is not consumed as the days progress. Stone is a volatile resource, it is subject to being destroyed through failure or bad luck.

Progress

Progress represents how close the reconstruction project is to completion. There are two counters, one for stonework and one for woodwork. Both must be filled to complete the game. Progress is a non volatile resource, it is not subject to being destroyed through failure or bad luck.
